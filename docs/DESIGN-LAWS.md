# The Design Laws

This product is built for operators with adult ADHD, cognitive dysfunction,
and visual-first minds — and it borrows deliberately from cognitive
behavioral therapy. These laws are the product's identity. Every feature,
page, and pixel must pass them. They are also the sales pitch.

## The architecture: Three Rooms and a Drawer

> "Three rooms. Your whole laundry business."

1. **🏰 Kingdom** (home) — *feel* the business before reading anything.
   One next action. Visible consequences. The war (Level 4) is reached
   through the kingdom's mythology, never as a parallel dashboard.
2. **🧺 Counter** — where the work gets done. Live pipeline is the spine;
   New order, History, Money owed, and Requests are views inside the same
   room. Money is daily work — it lives here, never in settings.
3. **🫂 People** — customers, leads, vendors. Everyone we serve, recover,
   sell to, and onboard.
4. **⚙ Drawer** — low-frequency configuration only: price list, sky/goal
   settings, the CFO Cockpit add-on, the reflection archive. Daily work
   never goes in the drawer.

## The laws

1. **One next action.** Every room answers "what should I do right now?"
   with exactly one loudest element. Decision paralysis is the enemy.
2. **No shame states.** No red badges as standing guilt. Counts are
   neutral; color appears only for genuinely time-sensitive exceptions.
   Bad days are weather, not verdicts (mercy floors, cozy-nest modes,
   regression toward neutral overnight).
3. **Wins are permanent.** A lit lantern never goes out. Acquisition
   cannot be un-happened. The kingdom remembers.
4. **Numbers live inside their graphics.** A number may only exist inside
   the visual it describes (stone tablet, gauge dial, scroll). State must
   be readable pre-verbally — position, color, posture, light — with
   digits as confirmation, not as the message.
5. **One truth, many views.** Tabs and rooms are lenses on the same state.
   Completing a pickup updates Live, History, the kingdom, and the war —
   the same object everywhere. Never two systems holding the same fact.
6. **Visible consequences within one second.** Every real action moves
   something on screen (the front line, a lantern, a runestone, the sky).
   Nothing animates without a real business event — and no real event
   passes silently.
7. **Progressive disclosure for math.** The full breakdown is always one
   tap away ("Show me the math", the scroll) and never shouts by default.
8. **Earned rewards only.** No variable-ratio slot-machine mechanics, no
   streak-snapping, no countdown anxiety. Momentum is rewarded (combo
   windows), never demanded.
9. **The operator owns the thermostat.** Thresholds that color the world
   (red/blue bars, campaign targets) are operator-tunable. The system
   pushes forward; it never buries.
10. **Old paths keep working.** Muscle memory and deep links survive every
    reorganization. Simplify navigation, never amputate capability.

## Why this sells

Competitors ship spreadsheets with feature lists. This ships a place the
operator *wants* to return to — a kingdom that visibly wakes as their
business grows, a war they can win every day, and an operational counter
with zero hunting. Retention is the product.
